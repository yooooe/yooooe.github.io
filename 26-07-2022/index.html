mc-toy 26 07 22 #notes

saying we need to focus on ‘gameplay’ as the crux of a needed avant garde in games is perhaps itself also a layover of a past medium? altgame stuff is often critiqued in the sense that it does very little with the ‘medium of games’ and are generally more concerned with being animation or cinema or paintings etc, but perhaps we can add ‘gameplay’ to that pile, with the historical precedent being non digital games themselves, that vgames at their core arent gameplay , but simply ‘interfaces’, and that gameplay or maybe a better term for it would be rules perhaps, is just an aspect of it that has proposed itself as the centre? i think about this precisely because ‘art content delivery games’ that have borrowed forms as ‘gameplay’ often still work as games, precisely becuase the ‘core of the medium’ is not gameplay, which is perhaps a false idol in a lot of ways, and is rather their quality as interfaces and more ‘purely’ their propulsive libidinal atmosphere? a game with rules based gameplay vs game with no rules based gameplay isnt the deciding factor being gamic avant garde vs non gamic / visual avant garde? they both remain in the circle of games, both equally ‘gamic’?

noise music often positioned as apex of radical aesthetics and I can feel why that is, but somehow still seems to flounder or be too happy rolling around in it’s own shit. in the same way a lot of academic writers position themselves and their ideas as radical, I don’t think that’s a particularly interesting or useful statement, as radical things can often easily fall to the wayside. noise music is a ‘pure outside’ or so outside to the point it ceases interfacing with the inside, which is for me where a lot of art, music, and ideas die. not in a poptimist way but in a way that acknlowedges the need for stuff like Fisher’s ‘popular modernism’– that really what instigates sea changes is in the post- movements which utilise the broken glass left on the floor by the ’true radicals’ to create something that shifts perceptions tangibly. noise music, and perhaps a vgame parrallel being certain strands of altgames a la pure indecipherabillity, ’the experimental genre work’ etc), often simply just define too well a new standard away from our current states, and just create new conformities; how well does a noise record or a field recording upset any sort of standard in current year? perhaps the only shock is that somehow people still present merzbow infatuation copies as the cutting edge– can imagine even my own position here being a justification (“look it pisses people off!”). im not pissed but it is sad to see experimentalism become further aesthetically codified, and even further sectioned off from where a popular modernism could emerge. a kind of neo-revolutionary attitude to art perhaps that we shouldn’t attempt to approach anything that could compromise the purity of experimentation. would reiterate again this isn’t advocating poptimism, as i think as has been proven in the comedown of pc music etc, that we end up just reifying bland trends (see: the persistence of 80s/90s/y2k wank in current pop still being portrayed as somehow interesting or new or anything beyond laziness).
