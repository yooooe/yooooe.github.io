mc-toy 26 07 22 #notes

saying we need to focus on ‘gameplay’ as the crux of a needed avant garde in games is perhaps itself also a layover of a past medium? altgame stuff is often critiqued in the sense that it does very little with the ‘medium of games’ and are generally more concerned with being animation or cinema or paintings etc, but perhaps we can add ‘gameplay’ to that pile, with the historical precedent being non digital games themselves, that vgames at their core arent gameplay , but simply ‘interfaces’, and that gameplay or maybe a better term for it would be rules perhaps, is just an aspect of it that has proposed itself as the centre? i think about this precisely because ‘art content delivery games’ that have borrowed forms as ‘gameplay’ often still work as games, precisely becuase the ‘core of the medium’ is not gameplay, which is perhaps a false idol in a lot of ways, and is rather their quality as interfaces and more ‘purely’ their propulsive libidinal atmosphere? a game with rules based gameplay vs game with no rules based gameplay isnt the deciding factor being gamic avant garde vs non gamic / visual avant garde? they both remain in the circle of games, both equally ‘gamic’?
